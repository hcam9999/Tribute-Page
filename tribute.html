Sir Alexander Fleming

The man responsible for discovering penicillin

Sir Alexander Fleming making a streak plate for microorganism culture.

Below is a time line of Sir Fleming's life:

  • August 6, 1881 - Alexander Fleming is born at Lochfield farm near Darvel in Ayrshire, Scotland.
  • 1903 - Alexander Fleming enrolls in medical school.
  • 1906 - Fleming graduates with distinction.
  • 1921 - Fleming discovers an important bacteriolytic substance he names Lysozyme.
  • September 28, 1928 - Fleming discovers the world's first antibiotic after mold he accidentally develops creates a bacteria-free circle around itself.
  • March 7, 1929 - Fleming names his finding "penicillin."
  • 1941 - Florey and Chain continue Fleming's research then begin mass producing penicillin after the bombing of Pearl Harbor.
  • 1945 - Along with Florey and Chain, Fleming is awarded the Nobel Prize in Medicine.
  • March 11, 1955 - Fleming dies of a heart attack in his London home. He is buried at St. Paul's Cathedral.
